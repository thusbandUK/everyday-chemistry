WHY NOW CHEMISTRY?

Most of my job is working out what a student knows right now. Why is that so important? It tells me what they don’t know, how I can teach it and then whether they’ve understood it.

Say they don’t know how ionic bonding works, but they know what they like on their chips. So we discuss what holds the crystals of salt together and now when I ask them, they know what ionic bonding is.

Rapport

I quickly establish rapport with students and maintain a space where they feel safe. I encourage them to make mistakes, because there’s no learning science without making mistakes, but then praise what is scientific about their insights. I find out what makes them tick and then I relate new concepts in the contexts of their hobbies, passions and ambitions.
